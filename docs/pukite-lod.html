LOD Tidal Factor Explorer

Multiple linear regression fit of lunar tidal sinusoids to the Length-of-Day (dLOD) time series. Amplitudes and phases are determined via MLR on the training window, then applied over the full record.

The MLR model uses 31 lunar tidal sinusoids from the lpap parameter set in experiments/Feb2026/11/lt.exe.p. Periods range from ≈6.8 days to ≈18.6 years. Fitting is performed only on the training window; the shaded region indicates the training period on the chart.

Tip: Multiple near-identical periods (eight ~27-day, five ~13.6-day, four ~9.1-day terms) are near-collinear in short training windows. Use ridge λ ≥ 0.1 or extend the training window to ≥ 1990 to achieve full-record CC > 0.9.

Data notes

LOD data (dLOD3.dat) represents the deviation of the Earth's rotation rate from a uniform reference, expressed in milliseconds. Source: GEM-LTE / experiments / dLOD3.dat . Tidal factor periods from the LTE model: experiments/Feb2026/11/lt.exe.p (lpap field).

Reference: Pukite, Mathematical Geoenergy (AGU/Wiley 2019). See also GeoEnergyMath.com.
